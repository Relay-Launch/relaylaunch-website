# RelayLaunch Market Landscape – Digital Infrastructure & AIOps Consulting (v1)

This document summarizes current market data and trends relevant to RelayLaunch LLC as a **digital infrastructure and AIOps-focused consultancy**, based primarily in the U.S. and targeting SMB and mid-market clients.

---

## 1. Big Picture: Digital & IT Consulting

- Global **digital consulting services** are estimated around **$500B in 2025**, with projections toward roughly **$1.2T by 2033** at **~12% CAGR**, driven by cloud adoption, AI, and cybersecurity demand.[web:116]
- The broader **IT consulting services** market is projected to grow at **~11–14% CAGR** through 2033, reflecting strong demand for digital transformation, cloud, and security advisory.[web:107][web:125]
- Within this, U.S. digital services consulting accounts for **tens of billions in annual spend** and remains the single largest country market, with North America holding ~37–40% share.[web:122][web:119]

**Implication for RelayLaunch:** You are entering a very large, growing market where even a tiny share (fractions of a percent) is meaningful in revenue terms. Differentiation around *digital infrastructure + AI-in-operations* is the key.

---

## 2. Cloud Managed & Infrastructure Services

RelayLaunch positions itself as a digital infrastructure consultancy that designs, builds, and operates cloud-native systems for clients. The relevant adjacent markets show strong growth:

- The **global cloud managed services market** was roughly **$134B in 2024** and is projected to reach about **$305B by 2030**, a **14.7% CAGR (2025–2030)**.[web:129]
- Another estimate pegs **cloud managed services** around **$155.7B in 2025**, growing to **$482.9B by 2034** at **13.4% CAGR**.[web:120]
- U.S. cloud managed services revenue alone was **$33.8B in 2024** and is expected to grow to **$64.2B by 2030** at **11.4% CAGR**.[web:123]

These markets are driven by:
- Migration to cloud and hybrid cloud architectures.
- Need for **managed security, network, and data-center services** as environments become more complex.[web:117][web:129]
- Desire to reduce in-house infrastructure staffing and focus on core business capabilities.[web:117]

**Implication for RelayLaunch:** Packages that offer **managed cloud infrastructure + observability + AIOps** (even if initially implemented via n8n, Supabase, Vercel, etc.) are well aligned with where spending is going.

---

## 3. AIOps & AI-in-IT-Operations

RelayLaunch’s “Control Center” product squarely overlaps the **AIOps market**, which focuses on using AI to automate and improve IT operations.

- One recent estimate values the global **AIOps market** at **$32.5B in 2025**, forecasting **$132.2B by 2034** at **~16.9% CAGR (2026–2034)**.[web:118]
- Another forecasts AIOps at **$16.6B in 2025** and **$85.4B by 2035**, a **17.8% CAGR (2026–2035)**.[web:121]
- A third study cites AIOps revenue around **$5.3B in 2024**, expecting **$44.1B by 2034** at **22.4% CAGR**, underscoring that, while estimates differ, **all see very rapid growth**.[web:130]

Key drivers:
- Explosion of telemetry from cloud-native, microservices, and distributed systems.
- Demand for **automated incident detection, root-cause analysis, and remediation**.
- Integration of AIOps into **DevOps and SRE workflows** and hybrid/multi-cloud environments.[web:118][web:130]

**Implication for RelayLaunch:** Framing the RelayLaunch Control Center as a **lightweight, opinionated AIOps + automation layer** specifically tailored to SMB/mid-market (rather than Fortune-500 scale) gives you a strong market story.

---

## 4. Digital Transformation & Strategy Consulting

RelayLaunch sits at the intersection of **digital transformation consulting** and **IT strategy consulting** for infrastructure-heavy clients.

- Global **digital transformation consulting** is estimated around **$383B in 2025**, expected to reach nearly **$896B by 2033** at **11.2% CAGR**.[web:125]
- U.S. digital transformation consulting alone was about **$77.5B in 2024**, with forecasts above **$245B by 2033**, a **13.7% CAGR (2025–2033)**.[web:119]
- IT strategy consulting provider services are expected to grow at **~10.7% CAGR** over the 2025–2032 period, driven by cloud, AI, and hybrid work models.[web:110]

**Implication for RelayLaunch:** Your consulting side (outside of the Control Center product) can confidently position itself within a high-growth digital/SaaS transformation market, using infrastructure modernization + automation as your wedge.

---

## 5. Managed IT & ICT Services

Many potential RelayLaunch clients might currently rely on or be considering **managed IT service providers (MSPs)**.

- The **managed IT services** market is projected to grow at about **14.2% CAGR** through 2032, driven by cybersecurity, remote work, and cloud adoption.[web:105]
- ICT managed services (a broader category including communications) are projected to grow at **9.6% CAGR**, as organizations increasingly outsource complex IT/communication stacks.[web:111]

These services often focus on **keeping the lights on**, but not necessarily on **advanced automation or AI-in-operations**, leaving room for a specialist partner like RelayLaunch.

**Implication for RelayLaunch:** You can partner with or even sell on top of existing MSP relationships by offering **AIOps, automation design, and control-center dashboards** that plug into their existing tooling.

## 6. Positioning Opportunities for RelayLaunch

### 6.1 Niche Positioning

Based on the above markets, a clear niche for RelayLaunch could be:

> "A digital infrastructure consultancy that designs, builds, and operates a **lightweight AIOps-style control center** for SMB and mid-market companies running on modern cloud platforms (Vercel, Supabase, AWS, etc.)."

Key differentiation angles:
- **Productized consulting:** Fixed-scope offerings around Control Center onboarding, automation design, and monthly optimization.
- **Opinionated stack:** Vercel, Astro, Node/TS, Supabase, n8n, with a future-proof path to AWS.
- **Outcome language:** "hours saved," "incidents prevented," "changes shipped per month" rather than raw infrastructure metrics.

### 6.2 Initial Target Segments

Consider focusing on:
- B2B SaaS companies with **5–50 engineers**, feeling pain around ops visibility and change requests.
- Agencies or service businesses increasingly building automations for their own clients and needing **centralized control/observability**.
- High-margin professional service firms (legal, consulting, boutique finance) undergoing digital transformation and willing to pay for **white-glove automation + oversight**.

---

## 7. Strategic Takeaways for the Control Center Build

- The **market growth rates (10–20% CAGR)** across digital consulting, cloud managed services, and AIOps support investing in a reusable product (Control Center) rather than purely bespoke consulting.[web:125][web:129][web:118]
- Buyers are overwhelmed by tooling; what many want is a **single, opinionated interface** (your Control Center) that surfaces the few metrics that matter and makes change requests & approvals simple.
- SMB/mid-market clients value **time-to-value** and **fixed pricing** more than deep customization; your Phase‑1 stack (Vercel + Supabase + n8n) is ideal for fast delivery.

- In your pitch and website copy, reference the **size and growth** of digital consulting, cloud managed services, and AIOps to show you are playing in a large, expanding space.
- In pricing discussions, anchor your value against the cost of

  - or paying for multiple disjointed vendor tools.
- Use growth projections to justify **multi-year partnerships** and roadmap conversations with clients.
