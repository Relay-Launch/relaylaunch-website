# RelayLaunch Control Center – Sales Deck Outline (v1)

This document provides a **slide-by-slide outline** for a RelayLaunch Control Center sales deck. It is designed for:

- Prospective SMB/mid‑market clients
- Partner agencies
- Light investor/partner conversations

Use this as the blueprint for a Keynote/Slides/PowerPoint deck. Pair it with:
- `cc-product-v1.md` – product one‑pager
- `relaylaunch-market-landscape-v1.md` – market data

---

## Slide 1 – Title & Credibility

**Title:**
- "RelayLaunch Control Center"
- Subtitle: "Digital Infrastructure & AIOps for Growing B2B Teams"

**Content:**
- RelayLaunch logo, your name, title, and contact info.
- 1‑line tagline: "A single control plane for your automations, AI agents, and digital infrastructure."

**Notes:**
- Keep this visually simple and brand‑aligned.

---

## Slide 2 – The Stakes (Why Now)

**Headline:**
- "Digital operations are exploding in complexity."

**Bullets:**
- Cloud, SaaS, and AI adoption are driving **double‑digit growth** in digital consulting and cloud managed services worldwide.[web:116][web:129]
- SMB and mid‑market teams are running more automations and AI agents than ever before.
- Leadership still asks basic questions like "What’s running? Is it working? What value are we getting?"

**Visual:**
- Diagram or collage of tools (CRMs, n8n, Vercel, AWS, AI APIs) with arrows everywhere.

---

## Slide 3 – The Problem

**Headline:**
- "Automation without control creates risk and confusion."

**Bullets:**
- No single source of truth for workflows, bots, and infrastructure.
- Opaque value – hard to prove ROI of automations and AI agents.
- Risky, ad‑hoc change processes via Slack threads and tickets.
- In-house DevOps/SRE is expensive and often overkill for SMBs.

**Speaker notes:**
- Relate to a real or anonymized client story ("We had 20+ automations and no idea which ones were still active").

---

## Slide 4 – Our Insight

**Headline:**
- "You don’t need more tools. You need a control center."

**Bullets:**
- SMB/mid‑market teams don’t want another complex observability suite.
- They need a **single, opinionated view** that answers:
  - What’s running?
  - Is it healthy?
  - What value is it creating?
  - How do we change it safely?

**Visual:**
- Before/after diagram: chaotic tools vs. one RelayLaunch Control Center screen.

---

## Slide 5 – RelayLaunch Control Center (Overview)

**Headline:**
- "A unified dashboard and control plane for your digital operations."

**Bullets (from product one‑pager):**
- Multi‑tenant dashboard per business or brand.
- Metrics for leads, automations, and hours saved.
- Workflow and AI agent inventory with health/status.
- Built‑in approvals and change requests.

**Visual:**
- Mockup or screenshot of the dashboard (using your seeded demo data).

---

## Slide 6 – Key Capabilities

**Headline:**
- "What you get with the Control Center."

**Three columns or sections:**

1. **Visibility**
   - Tenant dashboards with metrics and activity.
   - Inventory of workflows and AI agents.

2. **Governance**
   - Change request & approval workflows.
   - Quarterly change budgeting (small/medium/new).

3. **Reliability & Optimization**
   - Health status for key automations.
   - Continuous tuning by RelayLaunch as your infrastructure partner.

---

## Slide 7 – How It Fits Your Stack

**Headline:**
- "Works with your existing tools."

**Bullets:**
- Connects to your existing workflows (n8n, custom APIs, SaaS apps).
- Phase 1 built on modern cloud stack (Vercel, Supabase, Node/TS, Astro).
- Ready for AWS migration when you outgrow the starter stack.

**Visual:**
- Simple architecture diagram:
  - Left: client apps and SaaS tools → n8n → RelayLaunch API → Control Center UI.
  - Underneath: Supabase Postgres as the data store.

---

## Slide 8 – Customer Journey / Engagement Model

**Headline:**
- "From audit to ongoing operations."

**4-step horizontal timeline:**

1. **Discovery & Fit Check**
   - Short call, understand stack and goals.

2. **Audit & Design (Sprint 0)**
   - Map existing automations and data sources.
   - Define metrics and change budget.

3. **Implementation (Builds 1–3)**
   - Build 1: baseline dashboard.
   - Build 2: live metrics from Supabase + n8n.
   - Build 3: approvals + first live tenant.

4. **Operate & Optimize**
   - Monthly/quarterly reviews.
   - Add new automations and agents as your business evolves.

---

## Slide 9 – Pricing & Packages (Draft)

**Headline:**
- "Clear packages aligned to your stage."

**Three columns:**

1. **Starter**
   - 1 tenant, limited workflows/agents.
   - Defined monthly change budget.
   - Email support.

2. **Professional**
   - Multi‑tenant (for multi‑brand or agencies).
   - Larger change budget.
   - Slack support + quarterly review.

3. **Enterprise / Custom**
   - Custom SLAs.
   - Advanced monitoring & AWS migration work.

**Notes:**
- Leave pricing ranges open to customize per prospect; later you can plug in numbers.
